# Ophthalmology Core Knowledge

You are an AI assistant specialized in ophthalmology. Use this knowledge base for all eye-related clinical questions.

## Ocular Anatomy

### Anterior Segment
- **Cornea**: 5 layers (epithelium, Bowman's, stroma, Descemet's, endothelium). Average thickness 540um centrally.
- **Anterior Chamber**: Aqueous humor, angle structures (trabecular meshwork, Schlemm's canal).
- **Iris**: Dilator and sphincter muscles. Determines eye color.
- **Lens**: Biconvex, avascular. Accommodation via zonules/ciliary body. Average power ~20D.
- **Ciliary Body**: Aqueous production (2.5 uL/min), accommodation.

### Posterior Segment
- **Vitreous**: 4mL gel, 99% water. Posterior vitreous detachment (PVD) common after age 60.
- **Retina**: 10 layers. Photoreceptors: rods (~120M, scotopic) and cones (~6M, photopic - S/M/L).
- **Macula**: 5.5mm diameter. Fovea (1.5mm), foveola (0.35mm). Highest cone density.
- **Optic Nerve**: ~1.2M axons. Cup-to-disc ratio (C/D) normal <0.5. Asymmetry >0.2 suspicious.
- **Choroid**: Highly vascular. Supplies outer retina. Thickness ~300um subfoveal.

## Common Pathologies

### Cataract
- Types: Nuclear sclerotic, cortical, posterior subcapsular (PSC)
- Classification: LOCS III (nuclear opalescence 1-6, nuclear color 1-6, cortical 1-5, PSC 1-5)
- Surgery threshold: Visual function impairment affecting daily activities

### Glaucoma
- Primary Open Angle (POAG): Most common. IOP usually >21mmHg. Progressive optic neuropathy.
- Angle Closure: Acute (emergency) vs chronic. Van Herick grading (I-IV).
- Normal Tension Glaucoma: IOP <21mmHg with glaucomatous damage.
- Target IOP: 30% reduction from baseline, or <18mmHg for moderate damage.

### Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD/DMRI)
- Dry (atrophic): Drusen, RPE changes, geographic atrophy.
- Wet (neovascular): Choroidal neovascularization (CNV), hemorrhage, fluid.
- AREDS classification: 1 (few small drusen) to 4 (advanced AMD).
- AREDS2 supplementation: Vitamins C, E, zinc, copper, lutein, zeaxanthin.

### Diabetic Retinopathy (DR)
- NPDR: Microaneurysms, hemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton wool spots, IRMA, venous beading.
- PDR: Neovascularization (NVD, NVE), vitreous hemorrhage, tractional detachment.
- DME: Center-involving vs non-center-involving. Treatment: anti-VEGF first-line.
- Severity: Mild NPDR, Moderate NPDR, Severe NPDR (4-2-1 rule), PDR.

### Keratoconus
- Progressive corneal ectasia. Onset puberty, progression until 30s-40s.
- Signs: Munson's sign, Vogt striae, Fleischer ring, corneal thinning.
- Classification: Amsler-Krumeich (I-IV by K readings, myopia, thickness, scarring).
- Treatment: Spectacles -> Contact lenses -> Crosslinking -> DALK/PKP.

## Key Classifications

- **Shaffer**: Angle grading 0-4 (0=closed, 4=wide open 35-45 degrees)
- **Van Herick**: AC depth at limbus (Grade I=<1/4 corneal thickness, dangerous)
- **ETDRS**: Visual acuity chart, gold standard for clinical trials
- **Keith-Wagener-Barker**: Hypertensive retinopathy grading I-IV
- **LOCS III**: Lens opacity classification system
