# Ophthalmic Imaging Interpretation

## OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography)

### Macular OCT
- **Normal central foveal thickness**: 200-280 um (varies by device)
- **Layers to identify**: ILM, NFL, GCL, IPL, INL, OPL, ONL, ELM, IS/OS (ellipsoid zone), RPE
- **Key findings**:
  - Intraretinal fluid (cysts): DME, RVO, uveitis
  - Subretinal fluid: CSR, wet AMD, PCV
  - PED (pigment epithelial detachment): drusenoid, serous, fibrovascular
  - ERM (epiretinal membrane): wrinkling, traction
  - VMT (vitreomacular traction): partial vitreous separation with macular distortion
  - Macular hole: Stage 1-4 (Gass classification)

### RNFL OCT (Glaucoma)
- **Normal RNFL thickness**: ~100um average (varies by quadrant)
- **ISNT rule**: Inferior > Superior > Nasal > Temporal
- **Red flags**: Focal defects, RNFL thinning matching visual field loss
- **GCC (ganglion cell complex)**: Sensitive for early glaucoma detection

## Fundoscopy / Retinography

### Systematic Approach
1. **Optic disc**: Size, color, C/D ratio, neuroretinal rim, notching, hemorrhages
2. **Macula**: Foveal reflex, drusen, hemorrhage, edema, pigmentary changes
3. **Vessels**: A/V ratio (normal 2:3), AV nicking, tortuosity, neovascularization
4. **Periphery**: Lattice, holes, tears, detachment, pigmentation

### Diabetic Retinopathy Grading
- **Mild NPDR**: Microaneurysms only
- **Moderate NPDR**: More than microaneurysms, less than severe
- **Severe NPDR (4-2-1)**: >20 hemorrhages in 4 quadrants, OR venous beading in 2 quadrants, OR IRMA in 1 quadrant
- **PDR**: Neovascularization (disc or elsewhere), vitreous/preretinal hemorrhage

## Visual Fields (Campimetry)

### Humphrey Visual Field
- **Key indices**: MD (Mean Deviation), PSD (Pattern Standard Deviation), VFI (Visual Field Index)
- **GHT (Glaucoma Hemifield Test)**: Outside normal limits = suspicious
- **Reliability**: Fixation losses <20%, false positives <15%, false negatives <33%
- **Patterns**:
  - Arcuate scotoma: Glaucoma
  - Central scotoma: Macular disease, optic neuritis
  - Altitudinal defect: NAION, branch retinal artery occlusion
  - Bitemporal hemianopia: Chiasmal compression (pituitary)
  - Homonymous hemianopia: Post-chiasmal lesion (stroke)

### Progression Analysis
- **GPA (Guided Progression Analysis)**: Detects statistically significant change
- **MD trend**: Rate of change in dB/year. >-1.0 dB/year = fast progressor
- **VFI trend**: Rate of visual field index loss

## Topography / Corneal Tomography

### Normal Values
- Average K: 43-44 D
- Astigmatism: <1.5 D considered physiological
- Thinnest point: >500 um
- I-S value: <1.4 D (Rabinowitz criteria)

### Keratoconus Indicators
- Inferior steepening
- I-S value > 1.4 D
- Kmax > 47.2 D
- Thinnest point < 470 um
- Abnormal posterior elevation
- Belin/Ambrosio Enhanced Ectasia Display (BAD-D) > 1.6 SD

## Biometry (IOL Calculation)

### Measurements
- **Axial length (AL)**: Normal 22-25 mm. Short <22mm, long >25mm.
- **Keratometry (K)**: Average 43-44 D
- **ACD**: Anterior chamber depth, normal 2.5-3.5 mm
- **LT**: Lens thickness, normal 4-5 mm
- **WTW**: White-to-white, normal 11-12.5 mm

### Formula Selection
| Eye Length | Recommended Formulas |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Short (<22mm) | Hoffer Q, Barrett Universal II |
| Normal (22-25mm) | SRK/T, Holladay 1, Barrett |
| Long (>25mm) | SRK/T, Haigis, Barrett |
| Post-refractive | Barrett True-K, Shammas |
